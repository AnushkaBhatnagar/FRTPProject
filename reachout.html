Reach Out

Seeking help is important and should never be stigmatized. By reaching out, you'll help break the stigma associated with conditions like anxiety and depression. At the same time, you'll make sure that you find the right level of care and social support that you need.



For Women:


  • Helpline for Women In Distress - 1091

  • Helpline Domestic Abuse - 181

  • National Commission for Women Helpline (addressal of domestic violence, sexual violence and harassment cases).- 7827170170

  • Shakti Shalini Women's Shelter (supports survivors of gender and sexual violence) - (011) 24373736/ 24373737

  • Central Social Welfare Board (Police Helpline for empowerment of women and children) - 1091/ 1291, (011) 23317004

For Children:


  • Childline (for rescuing and assisting children in distress) - 1098

  • Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO- enables reporting of child sexual abuse and cyber crime case) - 9868235077

For Mental Health:


  • Vandrevala Foundation (for free psychological counseling and crisis intervention) - 1860 266 2345, 1800 233 3330

  • KIRAN (provides support to people facing anxiety, stress, depression, suicidal thoughts and other mental health concerns) - 1800-599-0019

  • iCall (for individuals in emotional and psychological distress) - 022-25521111, +91-9152987821

  • Mitram Foundation (suicide prevention helpline) - 80 2572 2573, +91 901 9708133

  • Sangath (free tele-counseling and self-management resources for frontline workers and those in need of psychological support amidst the pandemic) - 011-41198666

  • Fortis (for ensuring emotional health and well-being of students) - +91-8376804102
